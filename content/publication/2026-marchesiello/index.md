abstract: "Flash rips and surf eddies are transient 10–100 m structures that form in the surf zone without bathymetric variability. Whether they arise from individual waves or from wave groups remains an open question with direct implications for the use of wave-averaged, group-resolving models. We address this issue using CROCO in two configurations: a fully wave-resolving simulation and a wave-averaged simulation forced by the reconstructed wavegroup envelope. Diagnostics of mean and eddy fields reveal that group-resolving models successfully reproduce the dominant features of transient rips. This occurs because the eddy generation timescale falls within the infragravity range, while individual waves lack the coherence required to organize turbulence. Although the two models exhibit differences in eddy dynamics, group-resolving models realistically simulate eddy-induced transports at a fraction of the computational cost of wave-resolving models. Our findings clarify the physical mechanisms driving transient rips and validate group-resolving approaches for operational modeling."
